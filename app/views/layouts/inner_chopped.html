Multiple benefits

Multiple benefits are the advantages gained from retaining and restoring forests through REDD+, in addition to the protection and enhancement of carbon stocks that is the primary purpose of REDD+. This website focuses on the ecosystem-based benefits, including:

  • Soil conservation
  • Water regulation
  • Conservation of forest biodiversity
Provision of timber, forest foods and other non-timber forest products

For further information, see the Beyond Carbon: Ecosystem-based benefits of REDD+ brochure.

REDD+

REDD+ stands for “Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and forest Degradation plus the role of conservation, sustainable management of forests and enhancement of forest carbon stocks, in developing countries”. The concept is to create a financial value for the carbon stored in forests, supporting developing countries to reduce emissions from forested lands and invest in low-carbon paths to sustainable development. Financing options are being explored under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change. For further information see the UNFCCC website.

Social and Environmental Principles and Criteria

The UN-REDD Programme is developing a set of Social and Environmental Principles and Criteria in order to develop tools and guidance to enhance the multiple benefits of and reduce risks from REDD+. The Principles and Criteria are intended to support countries in operationalizing the UNFCCC guidance and safeguards for REDD+, and to provide the Programme with a framework to promote benefits and reduce risks from REDD+. The June 2011 draft can be accessed here.

UN-REDD Programme

The UN-REDD Programme is the United Nations Collaborative Initiative on REDD+. The Programme was launched in September 2008 to assist developing countries prepare and implement national REDD+ strategies, and builds on the convening power and expertise of the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO), the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) and the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP). For further information, see
http://www.un-redd.org/.
